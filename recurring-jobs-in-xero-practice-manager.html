Recurring Jobs In Xero Practice Manager: A Complete Guide

Introduction

Xero Practice Manager is an essential tool for accounting firms to manage their workflow and client jobs. One of the many features of Xero Practice Manager is the ability to create recurring jobs. Recurring jobs are tasks that need to be done on a regular basis, such as monthly payroll or quarterly BAS lodgements. In this article, we will discuss how to create and manage recurring jobs in Xero Practice Manager.

Why Create Recurring Jobs?

Recurring jobs can save a significant amount of time for accounting firms. Rather than creating a new job each time a task needs to be done, recurring jobs can automate the process. This can also help to ensure that tasks are completed on time, as reminders can be set up to notify team members when a job is due.

Creating a Recurring Job

To create a recurring job in Xero Practice Manager, navigate to the Jobs tab and click on the New Job button. From here, select the recurring job option and fill in the required details, such as the job name, client, frequency, and due date.

Frequency Options

Xero Practice Manager offers several frequency options for recurring jobs, including daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annually. You can also choose custom frequency options, such as every two weeks or every six months.

Setting Reminders

To ensure that team members are aware of when a job is due, reminders can be set up within Xero Practice Manager. This can be done by going to the Settings tab and selecting Reminders. From here, you can set up reminders for individual team members or for the entire team.

Managing Recurring Jobs

Once a recurring job has been created, it will automatically appear in the Jobs tab on the due date. From here, team members can complete the job and mark it as done. If changes need to be made to the recurring job, such as the due date or frequency, this can be done by editing the job details.

Deleting a Recurring Job

If a recurring job is no longer required, it can be deleted from Xero Practice Manager. To do this, navigate to the Jobs tab and select the job to be deleted. From here, click on the Delete button and confirm the deletion.

Conclusion

Recurring jobs are a powerful tool in Xero Practice Manager that can save accounting firms time and ensure that tasks are completed on time. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can create and manage recurring jobs in Xero Practice Manager with ease.
