What is an LPN?

An LPN is a licensed practical nurse who provides basic patient care under the supervision of a registered nurse or a physician. They play a vital role in the healthcare industry, and their responsibilities include monitoring patients' vital signs, administering medication, and providing basic patient care.

Education and Training

To become an LPN, you need to complete a practical nursing program, which usually takes about one year to complete. These programs are offered by vocational schools, community colleges, and some hospitals. After completing the program, you need to pass the National Council Licensure Examination (NCLEX-PN) to obtain a license to practice as an LPN.
