Introduction

Are you looking for a lucrative career in Sri Lanka? Have you considered a job as a Quantity Surveyor (QS)? If you're interested in this field but unsure about the salary prospects, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll explore everything you need to know about QS jobs in Sri Lanka, including the salaries, roles, and responsibilities.

What is a Quantity Surveyor?

A Quantity Surveyor is a professional who manages all aspects of construction costs. They are responsible for estimating, budgeting, and controlling the costs of a construction project. This includes everything from materials and labor to equipment and overheads. In Sri Lanka, Quantity Surveyors are in high demand, and the job market is growing rapidly.

Roles and Responsibilities

The roles and responsibilities of a Quantity Surveyor in Sri Lanka may vary depending on the organization they work for. However, some of the common tasks include:
  • Preparing cost estimates and budgets for construction projects
  • Advising clients on cost-effective solutions
  • Procuring materials and equipment
  • Managing contracts and payments
  • Preparing tender documents
  • Conducting site visits and inspections

Salary Prospects

Now, let's get to the most crucial question - how much can you expect to earn as a Quantity Surveyor in Sri Lanka? The salary depends on various factors such as your experience, qualifications, and the organization you work for. According to recent research, the average salary for a Quantity Surveyor in Sri Lanka is LKR 750,000 per annum.

Entry-Level QS Salaries

If you're just starting your career as a Quantity Surveyor in Sri Lanka, you can expect to earn around LKR 350,000 to LKR 500,000 per annum. This may vary depending on the organization and the location.

Experienced QS Salaries

If you have 5-10 years of experience in the field, you can earn around LKR 800,000 to LKR 1,200,000 per annum. Senior Quantity Surveyors with more than 10 years of experience can earn over LKR 2,000,000 per annum.

Skills and Qualifications

To become a Quantity Surveyor in Sri Lanka, you need to have a degree in Quantity Surveying or a related field. Some of the essential skills you need to possess include:
  • Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Excellent communication and negotiation skills
  • Good knowledge of construction materials and methods
  • Proficiency in project management software
  • Ability to work under pressure and meet deadlines

Job Opportunities

The job market for Quantity Surveyors in Sri Lanka is growing rapidly, and there are plenty of opportunities available in both the public and private sectors. Some of the popular organizations that hire Quantity Surveyors in Sri Lanka include:
  • Government agencies
  • Construction companies
  • Architectural firms
  • Engineering firms

Conclusion

In conclusion, if you're looking for a challenging and rewarding career in Sri Lanka, Quantity Surveying can be an excellent choice. With the growing demand for construction projects in the country, the job market for Quantity Surveyors is expanding rapidly. The salaries are competitive, and the career prospects are promising. So, what are you waiting for? Start exploring your options and take the first step towards a successful career in Quantity Surveying!
