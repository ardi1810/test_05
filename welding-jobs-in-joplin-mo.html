The Importance of Welding Jobs

Welding is a skilled trade that involves joining two or more metal parts using heat and pressure. It is a crucial process in many industries, such as automotive, construction, and manufacturing. Welders are in high demand due to the increasing need for metal fabrication and repair.

Overview of Welding Jobs in Joplin, MO

Joplin, MO is a city that has a thriving welding industry. The city is home to several companies that specialize in metal fabrication and welding, such as EaglePicher, Leggett & Platt, and Missouri Metals. These companies offer a wide range of welding jobs, from entry-level positions to high-paying, specialized roles.

Entry-Level Welding Jobs

Entry-level welding jobs in Joplin, MO typically require a high school diploma or equivalent and some welding experience. These jobs involve basic welding tasks, such as cutting and welding metal parts, operating welding equipment, and performing quality checks. The average salary for entry-level welding jobs in Joplin, MO is around $30,000 to $40,000 per year.

Specialized Welding Jobs

Specialized welding jobs in Joplin, MO require advanced welding skills and specialized knowledge. These jobs involve working with complex welding processes, such as TIG welding, MIG welding, and robotic welding. Specialized welding jobs also require certifications, such as the American Welding Society (AWS) certification. The average salary for specialized welding jobs in Joplin, MO is around $50,000 to $70,000 per year.

Benefits of Welding Jobs

Welding jobs offer several benefits for workers, such as job stability, career growth, and high earning potential. Welding is a skill that is in high demand, and the need for welders is expected to grow in the coming years. Welding jobs also offer opportunities for career growth, such as becoming a welding supervisor or a welding inspector.

How to Get Started in Welding

To get started in welding, you need to complete a welding training program. Many technical colleges and vocational schools offer welding programs that teach the fundamental skills and techniques of welding. These programs also provide hands-on training and practice to prepare students for entry-level welding jobs.

Conclusion

Welding jobs in Joplin, MO offer a range of opportunities for workers, from entry-level positions to specialized roles. Welding is a skilled trade that is in high demand, and it offers job stability, career growth, and high earning potential. If you are interested in welding, consider completing a welding training program to get started on a rewarding career path.
